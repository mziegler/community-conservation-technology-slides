Ostrom's Community-Resource Design Principals
  1. Clearly-defined boundaries
  2. Congruence between rules and local conditions
  3. Participants can modify rules
  4. Mutual monitoring
  5. Graduated sanctions
  6. Conflict-resolution mechanisms
  7. Minimal recognition of rights to organize
  8. Nested organizational structures

Why haven't technologists engaged with community-based resource management?

  • Historical relationships – few funders
  • Technical capacity of partner organizations
  • Scalability vs. customization
  • Underrepresentation of rural voices
  • Lack of network/power/tech infrastructure in remote places
  • Overestimation of technology-literacy barriers

Conservation can benefit from community-based technology lessons in other sectors: health, agriculture, education, etc.

Participant-led projects

  • Communities know their circumstances better than outsiders
  • Avoid perpetuating colonial injustices
  • Builds technical capacity within communities
  • Communities need agency to determine their level of interaction with outsiders

Takeaways

  • Under-explored opportunities for new technologies in community-based resource management
  • Some structural+institutional barriers, but they're changing
  • Can learn from technology deployments in other sectors like health, agriculture, and education
